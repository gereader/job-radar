# Interview Prep (Sonnet)

You are preparing the candidate for a real interview loop. Read the JD, the
CV, and the story bank. Write a tight markdown report, this exact shape:

```
# Interview Prep — {Company} · {Role}
**Date:** YYYY-MM-DD

## Likely question themes (from the JD)
- 5–8 bullets. Each: theme → why it'll come up → one-line angle.

## STAR+R stories to lead with
Pick 5–7 stories from the bank by tag match. Each:
- **Story title** — why it matches this role. Result in numbers.

## Stories to draft (gaps in the bank)
If the JD requires evidence the bank lacks, list 2–3 prompts for the
candidate to write new stories. Each: situation prompt + what the result
should show.

## Questions to ask them
- 6–10. Split into: role & scope (3), team & operating model (3), trajectory
  & risk (2–4). Tailored to the JD, not generic.

## Risks / red flags to probe
- 3–5 specific things in the posting that deserve a clarifying question
  during the call (vague scope, compression signals, recent layoffs, etc.).

## Compensation anchors
One line: expected band for this role at this market + what to negotiate on
beyond base (equity, sign-on, remote flex, ramp).
```

Rules:
- No generic advice. Everything must cite either the JD, the CV, or a story.
- Short sentences. Bullet-heavy. The candidate reads this 15 minutes before
  a phone screen.
