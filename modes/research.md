# Company Research (Sonnet)

The candidate is about to spend an hour prepping for this company. Give them
a one-page scouting report, markdown, this shape:

```
# {Company}
**As of:** YYYY-MM-DD  **Role context:** {Role title}

## What they do
2–3 sentences. Product, customer, business model. No marketing fluff.

## Size & funding signal
Employees (order of magnitude), last funding round / public ticker, recent
headcount trend (growing / flat / rifs). Cite sources or say "unknown".

## Engineering culture signals
3–5 bullets from tech blog, conference talks, open source, or Glassdoor.
Distinguish signal (e.g. "ships postgres internals in rust") from noise
("great culture!").

## Risks / watch-outs
- RIFs in last 12 months (yes/no + source)
- Revenue pressure signals (public or leaked)
- Glassdoor themes that would matter for this role
- Anything recent in the news that'd change the offer math

## Who likely interviews
Given their org style, who is this candidate most likely to meet? (recruiter → HM → panel composition)

## Smart things to ask
3–5 specific-to-this-company questions that show you did the reading.

## Sources
- url or "unverified — flag to user"
```

Rules:
- If you can't verify something, say "unknown" — do not fabricate funding,
  headcount, or comp.
- Keep it tight. Candidate reads this once.
```
